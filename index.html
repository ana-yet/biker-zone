Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To check if your helmet needs replacing: replace it every 3-5 years, look for visible damage (cracks, dents, fraying straps), ensure it fits snugly, and verify it meets current safety standards.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

To ensure your bike is safe to ride after storage, check the tires for wear or cracks, ensure the brakes function properly, inspect the chain and gears for rust or damage, and verify that all bolts are tightened. It’s also a good idea to take it for a quick test ride to ensure everything feels right. Happy and safe biking!

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet, obey traffic laws, use lights and hand signals, ride with traffic, stay alert, use bike lanes, and avoid distractions. Ride safe!
